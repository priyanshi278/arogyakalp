# ArogyaKalp: Frontend & Dataset Test Cases

This document provides sample inputs (Clinical Notes/Messages) to test the ArogyaKalp system on the frontend. Use these cases to verify the Named Entity Recognition (NER), ADR Detection, Drug Interaction (DDI) logic, and Recommendation modules.

---

### Scenario 1: Single Drug Side Effect Detection (ADR)
**Input:** "I am taking paracetamol for my fever, but I'm feeling a bit nauseous."
**Dataset Match:** `adr_dataset.csv` (paracetamol -> nausea, liver damage, skin rash)
**Expected Frontend Behavior:**
- **NER:** "paracetamol" should be highlighted as a DRUG.
- **Side Effects:** Displays "nausea", "liver damage", "skin rash" under potential side effects.
- **Analysis:** Chat response should confirm the side effect "nausea" is known for paracetamol.

---

### Scenario 2: Dangerous Drug-Drug Interaction (DDI)
**Input:** "Currently taking ibuprofen and warfarin. Is this combination safe?"
**Dataset Match:** `ddi_dataset.csv` (ibuprofen + warfarin -> dangerous)
**Expected Frontend Behavior:**
- **Hazard Banner:** A red "🚨 HIGH RISK INTERACTION DETECTED" banner should appear at the top.
- **Interactions Table:** Should show a "Dangerous" interaction between Ibuprofen and Warfarin.
- **Results:** Visual indicators (badges/icons) should clearly mark this as high-risk.

---

### Scenario 3: Drug Recommendation & Alternatives
**Input:** "I need an alternative for amoxicillin as I am allergic to it."
**Dataset Match:** `drug_alternatives.csv` (amoxicillin -> azithromycin for infection)
**Expected Frontend Behavior:**
- **Recommendations Section:** Should suggest "azithromycin" as an alternative for "infection".
- **Chat Response:** Should explain that azithromycin can be used instead of amoxicillin for infections.

---

### Scenario 4: Multiple Drugs & Conditions (Complex Note)
**Input:** "Patient is on lisinopril for hypertension and aspirin for heart health. They also reported using ibuprofen for pain recently."
**Dataset Match:** 
- `ddi_dataset.csv`: (lisinopril + ibuprofen -> caution), (aspirin + ibuprofen -> risky), (lisinopril + aspirin -> risky)
- `drug_alternatives.csv`: (lisinopril -> amlodipine)
**Expected Frontend Behavior:**
- **Multiple Interaction Rows:** Should show "Caution" and "Risky" rows for the different drug pairs.
- **NER Summary:** Should identify "lisinopril", "aspirin", and "ibuprofen".
- **Alternatives:** Suggest "amlodipine" for lisinopril if hypertension management is mentioned.

---

### Scenario 5: Safe Interaction
**Input:** "Can I take paracetamol and caffeine together?"
**Dataset Match:** `ddi_dataset.csv` (paracetamol + caffeine -> safe)
**Expected Frontend Behavior:**
- **Interactions Table:** Should show "Safe" or "No significant interaction found".
- **Chat Response:** Confirms it is safe to take these together.

---

### Scenario 6: Edge Case - Unknown Drug
**Input:** "I am taking Xyzzy-200 for my headache."
**Expected Frontend Behavior:**
- **NER:** Should likely fail to identify "Xyzzy-200" (heuristic/model limitation).
- **Chat Response:** "I couldn't identify any specific drugs in your message. Please provide the generic or common brand name."
- **Empty Results:** No side effects or interactions should be displayed.

---

### Scenario 7: Edge Case - Case Sensitivity & Formatting
**Input:** "i take ASPIRIN daily. can i take ALBUTEROL?"
**Dataset Match:** `adr_dataset.csv` (aspirin), `ddi_dataset.csv` (none for this pair specified as dangerous, but NER should pick them up).
**Expected Frontend Behavior:**
- **Normalization:** The system should correctly identify "ASPIRIN" and "ALBUTEROL" despite the casing.
- **NER:** Shows both drugs in the list.

---

### Scenario 8: Common Brand Names (Mapped to Generics)
**Input:** "I take Dolo 650 for my headache."
**Logic:** Backend maps brand names like Dolo 650 to paracetamol.
**Expected Frontend Behavior:**
- **NER:** Identifies "Dolo 650".
- **Analysis:** Displays side effects for "paracetamol".
- **Message:** "Detected paracetamol (found as Dolo 650)..."

---

### Scenario 9: High Risk Warning
**Input:** "I take aspirin and clopidogrel."
**Dataset Match:** `ddi_dataset.csv` (aspirin + clopidogrel -> dangerous)
**Expected Frontend Behavior:**
- **Danger Badge:** Visible red alert for dangerous interaction.
- **Chat Response:** Warns about the high risk of bleeding when combining these two antiplatelet drugs.
